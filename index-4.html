01 — workflow

How the pipeline works

STEP 1

Clean the data

Missing values handled, data types corrected, and categorical fields encoded into a model-ready format.

STEP 2

Explore patterns

EDA surfaces which customer behaviors correlate with churn — contract type, tenure, charges, and support usage.

STEP 3

Train models

Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosting are trained on the same split for fair comparison.

STEP 4

Evaluate & select

Each model is scored on accuracy, precision, and recall, and the strongest performer is kept for prediction.

04 — signals

What drives churn most

Contract type

Month-to-month customers churn far more than those on annual contracts.

Tenure

Customers in their first year are the most likely to leave.

Monthly charges

Higher bills correlate with higher churn, especially without long-term contracts.

Tech support usage

Repeated support tickets often signal unresolved frustration before cancellation.

Internet service

Fiber-optic customers show a different churn pattern than DSL customers.

Payment method

Electronic check users churn more than those on automatic payments.
